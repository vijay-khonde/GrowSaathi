🌱 Crop Rotation with Legumes (फसल चक्र)

Crop rotation is the agricultural practice of growing different crops in sequence on the same land to maintain soil quality. Including legumes (दलहनी फसलें) like moong, gram, peas, or groundnut fixes atmospheric nitrogen in the soil naturally, saving fertilization costs.

💧
Moisture Retention

Improves soil organic structure and water-holding properties.

🌿
Nitrogen Fixation

Roots host Rhizobium bacteria that naturally accumulate nitrogen.

🐛
Pest Disruption

Breaks life cycles of insect pests and soil diseases.

💰
Cost Reduction

Lowers synthetic chemical input budgets by up to 50%.

Actionable Sowing Steps

  1. Prepare a 2–3 year crop list based on local monsoon predictions.
  2. Select region-compatible legumes (Moong, Chickpea, Pea).
  3. Treat legume seeds with Rhizobium inoculum culture before sowing.
  4. Leave root residues in soil after harvest to decay into bio-nitrogen.
  5. Alternate dense root crops with shallow root grains to balance moisture.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will I observe soil structure improvements?
Usually within 1–2 full harvest seasons. Farmers generally report improved water retention, softer texture, and noticeably greener shoots with lower urea inputs.
Which legumes grow best in low-water regions?
Bengal Gram (Chana), Pigeon Pea (Arhar/Tur), and Cowpea (Lobia) are highly drought-tolerant, flourishing in drylands across Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, and interior Maharashtra.
Can I plant the same legume consecutive years?
It is not recommended. Repeating the same legume family consecutively increases the buildup of specific soil-borne root pathogens. Switch to alternative pulse families or cereal rotations.
Do I completely stop adding urea/nitrogen?
Not completely, but legume rotational plots require up to 60% less chemical nitrogen fertilization than monocropped cereal plots. Follow recommendations given by soil health cards.
